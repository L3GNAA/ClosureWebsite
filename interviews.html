Karine Citadel:

Well, project management is the backbone of nearly everything I do, professionally and personally. In my work, I use it to structure projects from start to finish: defining objectives, planning timelines, allocating resources, and coordinating with different stakeholders. It keeps me organized and sane; I think that's the most important part. It really keeps me sane, and it ensures that nothing falls through the cracks, and I like that. I like to have the precision and the organization, or really plan ahead so I can perform better.

In my personal life, project management shows up in the way I plan major transitions, manage finances, organize travel, and even set long-term goals. Breaking big objectives into smaller tasks is also something that it helps me with as well, along with setting deadlines and monitoring progress. All of that really gives me the clarity and the control over whatever it is that I'm doing. Overall, I would say that project management helps me make better decisions, anticipate problems, and stay accountable to my commitments.

Angel Contreras:

What advice do you have for me and my classmates who are learning about project management?

Karine Citadel:

I would say to start by mastering the basics: scope, time, resources, and risks. Know that these fundamentals never change. No matter how complex a project becomes, you are always going to have those fundamentals, and I think it's important for you to master them.

I would also say don't fall into the trap of thinking that project management is just a tool. As I explained before, you can use it in your everyday life; you can use it for everything, really. It's really about people, communication, clarity, and expectation setting, and I think those are just more important than software, I would say.

What else can I tell you? It will teach you how to learn to think ahead of any type of commitment. You are going to have to be proactive in everything that you do, and it's going to become a habit. So much so that you won't even have to think about the fundamentals anymore as far as specific steps; it will just be implemented into your actions.

Strong project managers are always scanning for risks and preparing solutions before a problem actually arises. I think this is something that you build over time because you always want to be ahead of everything. By thinking about all the scenarios that can present themselves, you can actually anticipate what is going to come towards you, and you will be better at it. You will have a better reputation by just being that person that people go to when it comes to planning, because you already know what can possibly happen and how to take care of that possible issue that can arise.

Finally, I would say stay curious because every project will teach you something different, something new about leadership, teamwork, and human behavior. That is an important one. The more adaptable and open you are, the stronger you will be in this field.
